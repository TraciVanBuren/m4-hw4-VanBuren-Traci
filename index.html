Biography

Prince was a rockstar and musical genius who rose to fame in the 1980s. He was born in Minneapolis, Minnesota in 1958. His mother was a singer and his father was a band leader/musician. He originally aspired to be a professional basketball player, but that never materialized because at 5'4, he was too short. Instead, he focused on music.

In 1984, he starred in the movie Purple Rain, which won an Academy Award for its score and its theme song "Purple Rain".

Prince married Mayte Garcia in 1996, but that marriage ended in divorce. He later married Manuela Testolini, but they also ended up divorcing.

He died in April 2016 from a drug overdose.

Discography

Prince's first album was called For You and was released in 1978.

His next album to garner acclaim was 1999 and it contained the epic single "1999," which became an anthem to the turn of the millenium and his breakout album. "1999" was created with his new band, The Revolution.

Purple Rain

Purple Rain, the movie and album, is so influential, that it gets its own subheading. During the iconic summer of 1984, Prince wrote the music and starred in the musical movie masterpiece titled "Purple Rain." It became a runaway hit and part of the musical canon. The movie resulted in several singles including "I would die 4 U," "When Dove's Cry," and the ballad "Purple Rain".
